===== 03. Microtonal & Xenharmonic Sets =====

==== 19-EDO ====
<code>Division: 19 equal steps per octave.
Characteristic: Greatly improved "pure" minor thirds and major sixths compared to 12-tet.</code>

==== 22-EDO ====
<code>Division: 22 equal steps per octave.
Characteristic: Excellent "super-major" and "sub-minor" intervals; very distinct "alien" feel.</code>

==== 31-EDO (Huygens-Fokker) ====
<code>Division: 31 equal steps per octave.
Characteristic: Very close to 1/4 comma meantone; allows for "septimal" (harmonic 7th) intervals.</code>

==== Bohlen-Pierce Scale ====
<code>Division: Based on the "tritave" (ratio of 3:1) divided into 13 steps.
Characteristic: No octaves! A truly experimental landscape.</code>

===== 07. Compositional Challenges =====

==== The Tritone Scavenge ====
<code>Constraint: Every melodic interval must be an augmented 4th (tritone). Chords must contain at least one tritone.</code>

==== The Hollow Bone (Monophony) ====
<code>Constraint: A single melodic line only. No harmony, no chords, no double-tracking. Focus on rhythm and phrasing.</code>

==== The Murder's Flight (Polyrhythm) ====
<code>Constraint: Use 3 against 4 or 5 against 7 patterns throughout the piece to mimic chaotic bird flight.</code>

==== Corvid Vocalisation (Mimicry) ====
<code>Constraint: Use portamento (slides) and microtonal bends to mimic the "caw" and "click" sounds of a crow.</code>

==== The Shiny Object (Ostileto) ====
<code>Constraint: One short, high-pitched melodic phrase must repeat (loop) through the entire song regardless of key changes.</code>

==== Twelve-Tone Row (Serialism) ====
<code>Constraint: Use all 12 notes of the chromatic scale in a specific order. No note can repeat until all 12 are played.</code>
